The Drift Diffusion Model

Mapping the Architecture of Choice

How does the brain decide what we perceive? The Drift Diffusion Model (DDM) reveals the neural mechanisms of perceptual decisionsâ€”particularly how the brain accumulates noisy sensory evidence over time until reaching a decision threshold.

Watch the random dot motion stimulus and see how the brain's "evidence accumulator" integrates information until it commits to a choice.

  • Coherence controls the drift rate (signal strength)
  • Threshold determines the speed-accuracy tradeoff
  • Noise adds randomnessâ€”the source of errors
  • Based on research by Newsome, Shadlen & Ratcliff
